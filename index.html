SustainableU

by Hanna Co

This project will be focused on how we as individuals can contribute to sustainable city living. Many sites talk about sustainability, but often are looking on a larger scale, which can discourage the viewer or give them the feeling that they are alleviated from individual responsibility to live more sustainably. Thus, I want to design a site that explains these issues at a large scale, but gives the viewer action items they can do to take steps towards sustainable living.

Design Statement

How might I design a website that aids users in building sustainable cities and communities?

Many of our cities were not built with sustainablity in mind, with inadequate public transit infrastructure, and often become filled with litter and garbage over time. On the individual level, there are many sites that attempt to mobilize members of the community to do their part in making their city more sustainable. However, many of these sites are too general, or only propose solutions that aren't accessible for average individuals. Additionally, some of these sites don't educate the user on sustainability, or only provide articles that many may skim over.

Thus, my project aims to not only provide users with interactive and fun ways to learn about sustainability, but also give ways that they can get invovled. While my site primarily targets college students, this site can be used by individuals of all ages and from various backgrounds. I wanted to create a website that motivates users to be proactive in efforts to promote sustainability in their communities.

Competitor Analysis

To get an idea of how other websites were presenting information and getting users involved, I conducted heuristic evaluations on two similar websites: United Nations Environment Programme and Global Environment Facility. I chose these sites because their goal was similar to mine for my website. I used Jakob Nielson's 10 Usability Heuristics to evaluate these sites and understand their importance and role in my own project.

From my evaluation, I found that what was often confusing was how much information they attempted to present at once, and how overwhelming this could be. In addition, I prioritized consistency of design throughout my site.

User Research

I wanted to take a step back from the specifics of my project, and focus on the user's general goals and viewws. I asked a friend of mine to help me in understanding what the average college student thought about sustainability.

Conducting user research allows me a glimpse into what others thought about sustainability and getting involved, which brought up some new issues I hadn't considered, such as how the user's perceived impact of their actions affect their interest level.

Personas and Scenarios

An integral component of the design process is to understand the user's perspective, from their goals and motivations to their emotions and thoughts. To accomplish this, I created personas, empathy maps, and scenarios to understand the user, and to also filter out personal preference. Doing so allows us to focus on the function of our product and and focus on the user's needs and goals.

Using the results of the contextual inquiry, I created a couple personas that captured my target demographic, as well as a few scenarios that outlined how someone might use my site.
